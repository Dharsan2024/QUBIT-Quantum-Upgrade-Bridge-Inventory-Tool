## Abstract

The rapid advancement of quantum computing poses a significant threat to existing public-key cryptographic systems, enabling future adversaries to exploit the Harvest Now, Decrypt Later (HNDL) strategy by storing encrypted data today for decryption once cryptographically relevant quantum computers become available. This emerging risk threatens critical sectors such as government, healthcare, finance, and cloud infrastructure, making proactive migration to post-quantum cryptography essential. QUBIT (Quantum Upgrade Bridge & Inventory Tool) is proposed to address this challenge by providing an intelligent, automated, and open-source framework for identifying vulnerable cryptographic assets and facilitating secure migration to quantum-resistant algorithms. Existing solutions primarily offer migration guidelines or expensive commercial products but lack integrated risk assessment, automated cryptographic inventory, migration prioritization, and hybrid transition support. QUBIT bridges these gaps by combining cryptographic asset discovery, Bayesian risk quantification, Monte Carlo-based quantum timeline estimation, AI-assisted migration planning, and a hybrid classical–post-quantum cryptographic bridge within a unified platform. The proposed framework enables organizations to prioritize vulnerable assets, generate migration recommendations, and ensure a smooth transition while maintaining operational continuity. By improving cryptographic visibility, reducing migration complexity, and strengthening long-term cybersecurity resilience, QUBIT supports organizations in preparing for the post-quantum era through a scalable, cost-effective, and research-driven solution.
